Local Energy Field

A decentralized system where energy is generated, stored, and consumed within a specific community, striving for greater independence and sustainability.

Overview

A local energy field refers to a decentralized system where energy is generated, stored, and consumed within a specific community or geographic area, rather than being supplied by a centralized national grid. These systems often involve citizens and local organizations, aiming for greater energy independence, more efficient resource use, and a more equitable transition to sustainable energy. This can be supported by localized planning and the use of technologies like solar panels, batteries, and smart grids.

Key Characteristics

Decentralized

Energy is produced close to where it is used, reducing transmission losses and reliance on large, remote power plants.

Community Owned

Systems are often owned or managed by local shareholders or members, more value-driven than profit-driven.

Integrated Tech

Local energy systems integrate generation (solar, wind), storage (batteries), and consumption seamlessly.

Citizen Participation

Empowers individuals to take an active role, promoting civic engagement and participatory consumption models.

Flexible & Resilient

By balancing generation and consumption locally, these systems adapt to local needs and increase resilience.

Local Planning

Considers specific geography, building stock, and social context for a coherent decarbonization strategy.

Broader Implications & Goals

  • Energy Sovereignty: Reduces dependence on external energy markets and price fluctuations.
  • Just Transition: Can help combat energy poverty by providing more affordable energy and ensuring that the benefits of the energy transition are shared more equitably across a community.
  • Economic & Social Transformation: Represents a shift from a consumerist relationship with energy to a more participatory and responsible one.
