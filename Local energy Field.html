Local Energy

A local energy field refers to a decentralized system where energy is generated, stored, and consumed within a specific community or geographic area, rather than being supplied by a centralized national grid. These systems often involve citizens and local organizations, aiming for greater energy independence, more efficient resource use, and a more equitable transition to sustainable energy. This can be supported by localized planning and the use of technologies like solar panels, batteries, and smart grids.

Key characteristics of local energy fields:



  • Decentralized generation: Energy is produced close to where it is used, reducing transmission losses and reliance on large, remote power plants.
  • Community ownership and control: Systems are often owned or managed by local shareholders or members, who can be more value-driven than profit-driven.
  • Integration of technologies: Local energy systems integrate generation (e.g., solar, wind), storage (e.g., batteries), and consumption.
  • Citizen participation: They empower individuals to take an active role in their energy supply, promoting civic engagement and a more participatory model of energy consumption.
  • Flexibility and resilience: By balancing generation and consumption at a local level, these systems can adapt to local needs and increase resilience to disruptions.
  • Local energy planning: To be effective, local energy systems benefit from a "local area energy plan" that considers the specific geography, building stock, and social context of the community to develop a coherent, data-driven strategy for decarbonization.

Broader implications and goals:

  • Energy sovereignty: Reduces dependence on external energy markets and price fluctuations.
  • Just transition: Can help combat energy poverty by providing more affordable energy and ensuring that the benefits of the energy transition are shared more equitably across a community.
  • Economic and social transformation: Represents a shift from a consumerist relationship with energy to a more participatory and responsible one.
