Alternate Universe

A hypothetical, separate universe that may coexist with our own, often existing in a multiverse of infinite possibilities.

Overview

An alternate or parallel universe is a hypothetical, separate universe that may coexist with our own, often existing in a multiverse. While this concept is a popular staple in science fiction, it is also a topic of serious discussion in theoretical physics, stemming from ideas in quantum mechanics, string theory, and cosmic inflation.

Scientific Theories

  • Cosmic Inflation: Some theories propose that regions of space are expanding at different rates. One model suggests that new "bubble universes" are constantly being created as part of this ongoing inflation.
  • Quantum Mechanics: The "many-worlds interpretation" suggests that every time a quantum event has multiple possible outcomes, the universe branches into separate universes, with each outcome occurring in its own reality.
  • String Theory: This theory allows for the possibility of other universes existing alongside our own, potentially governed by different physical laws.

In Fiction

  • Alternate universes are often used as a setting for stories where events unfold differently than in our reality.
  • These universes can be a minor variation, where a single event (like a historical war's outcome) is different, or they can be radically different, with unique physical laws or dominant species.
  • The concept is explored through alternate timelines, where events diverge from a specific point, or through entirely separate realities that are simply parallel to the "main" universe.

Key Differences & Concepts

  • Multiverse: The multiverse is the theoretical collection of all possible universes. It is the overarching concept that includes all alternate and parallel universes.
  • Different Levels: Some theoretical frameworks propose different "levels" of parallel universes, ranging from other parts of our own infinite universe to universes with entirely different physical constants.
